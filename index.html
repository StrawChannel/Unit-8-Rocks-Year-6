A full explanation for Unit 8.

Earth's Layers

Crust (5-70 km) - Solid rock surface
Mantle (2,900 km) - Semi-solid silicate rock
Outer Core (2,200 km) - Liquid iron/nickel
Inner Core (1,220 km) - Solid iron/nickel

Igneous Rocks

Formed from cooling magma (intrusive) or lava (extrusive)

Sedimentary Rocks

Formed from compressed sediments

Metamorphic Rocks

Formed from existing rocks under heat/pressure

How Layers Influence the Rock Cycle

Crust: Where weathering/erosion creates sediments and where magma cools to form igneous rocks

Mantle: Source of magma through partial melting (forms igneous rocks when cooled)

Core: Heat drives mantle convection that causes tectonic movement and metamorphism
